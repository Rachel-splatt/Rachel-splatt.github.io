UX design for kids

I’ve been teaching math for over a year and a half now, and am always in awe at the educational apps the kids bring in for their homework. Some of the kids homework assignments actually reward them with coins for getting the right answers, which I think is so cool! Others seem to encourage an endless cycle of “guessing and checking” until the student stumbles on a correct answer.

Education is something I am passionate about, so over the course of my Technology in the Youth Class, i took to writing a research papers on app design for children and educational technology.

On my own time, I thought it would be fun to translate what I learned into a multiplication fluency app— and so we have Emmy the Multiplication Monster.

It’s worth noting that this project is strictly based on research and my experience in teaching. No user tests were conducted, but, if I ever wanted to continue this project, this would be a must!

questions i'm left with

  • Is the app age appropriate? Other apps for this age group appear to have a similar feel, but is feeding a cartoon monster to juvenile for this age group?
  • Is 20 seconds an optimal amount of time to give before suggesting help on a question?
  • How can specific multiplication strategies be animated in a way that makes sense to kids?
  • Does the overal flow and reward system in the app make sense and appeal to the user?
